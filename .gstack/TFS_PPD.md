# Building the data-and-digital narrative for Thermo Fisher's Clinical Research Group

**Thermo Fisher Scientific's PPD division is in the middle of its most aggressive digital transformation ever**, anchored by a $8.875B Clario acquisition, an OpenAI partnership deploying AI across clinical operations, and a Datavant data-linkage collaboration — all announced within a five-month window (October 2025 – February 2026). These moves aim to close a meaningful data-and-technology gap with market leader IQVIA while leveraging PPD's unique "molecule-to-medicine" integration that no pure-play CRO can replicate. The CRO market itself has ballooned to **$80–92 billion** with digital capabilities becoming the primary competitive battleground, as 80%+ of trials miss enrollment timelines and each day of delay costs roughly **$500K in lost revenue**. For a Data/Digital/Technology candidate interviewing within the CRG, the strategic narrative is clear: PPD's path to differentiation runs through data unification, AI-driven trial optimization, and end-to-end platform integration.

## The $92B CRO market is a data arms race

The global CRO services market reached an estimated **$80–92 billion in 2025** (Fortune Business Insights places it at $92.3B), growing at a **7.5–9.5% CAGR** toward $130–199B by the early 2030s. Clinical services dominate with **75–78% market share**, oncology accounts for **31–35%** of therapeutic area spend, and North America represents 35–50% of global revenue.

What's changed in recent years is how CROs compete. The historical model of competing on geographic footprint and therapeutic expertise has given way to technology-led differentiation. IQVIA holds a **33% share** of global health analytics and deploys **150+ AI agents** in production. Parexel partnered with Palantir to build an AI-native clinical data platform, achieving an **85% reduction in data engineering costs** and **70% faster time-to-market** for data products. ICON launched a tokenization solution matching **~90% of trial participants** to real-world data sources. Even Labcorp's Drug Development arm built snapClinical, a proprietary DCT platform. In this environment, PPD's data and AI investments are not discretionary — they are existential.

The decentralized clinical trial (DCT) market, a key enabler of digital CRO services, reached **$8.8–9.4B in 2025** and is growing at **13–15% CAGR**. The FDA's September 2024 finalized guidance on conducting trials with decentralized elements was a major catalyst. The real-world evidence (RWE) solutions market sits at approximately **$2.5–5.4B**, growing **9–16% annually**, and received a landmark boost in December 2025 when the FDA announced it would accept de-identified real-world evidence without always requiring identifiable patient data.

## PPD's digital arsenal is expanding fast

PPD operates a layered digital product portfolio spanning AI-powered analytics, decentralized trial infrastructure, and real-world evidence — all of which have been significantly expanded since 2024.

**Core AI suites.** PPD now markets four proprietary AI-powered product families. The **Clinical Trial Forecasting Suite** uses deep learning and proprietary performance data to predict milestones, streamline site selection, and forecast enrollment, with a 2025+ roadmap targeting fully automated site list finalization and AI-guided chat for decisions. The **Intelligent Clinical Suite** — described as "first-of-its-kind" — optimizes data collection and accelerates database lock through automated query resolution. The **Clinical Decision Suite** provides an AI-driven end-to-end view integrating milestone monitoring, risk prediction, and material management. And **AI Medical Writing** combines AI-assisted authoring with human oversight to accelerate submission-ready regulatory documents.

**Preclarus platform.** PPD's proprietary, CIO 100 Award-winning data platform consolidates and standardizes data from EDC, IVRS/IWRS, and central lab systems into real-time dashboards for project management, site performance, and patient monitoring. It supports risk-based monitoring, adaptive trial design, and includes a **virtual biorepository** with chain-of-custody tracking.

**SMART Pre-screening** uses AI-powered health record extraction to evaluate patient records against inclusion/exclusion criteria and produces a "probability-to-enroll" rating. **StudyGage**, a proprietary patient burden tool, leverages **60,000+ patient study participation decisions** to optimize protocol design. **TrialMed SOMS** provides a comprehensive site operations management suite covering CTMS, eSource, regulatory, and patient payments.

**Decentralized trial ecosystem.** PPD's DCT capabilities include eConsent, eCOA/ePRO, wearables integration, medication tracking, remote televisits, and PPD Home Trial Services (PPD-employed healthcare providers conducting blood draws and vital signs monitoring at patients' homes). The Science 37 partnership provides the SaaS-based technology backbone. PPD was recognized as an **ISG Provider Lens Leader** for digital transformation in clinical development in both 2023 and 2024.

## Three transformative deals in five months reshaped the CRG's digital trajectory

The most important strategic developments for a Data/Digital narrative at PPD occurred in rapid succession between October 2025 and February 2026.

**OpenAI partnership (October 2025).** Thermo Fisher selected PPD clinical research as the **initial high-impact focus area** for embedding OpenAI APIs across product development, service delivery, and operational efficiency. The projected impact is **25–60% time savings in clinical trial setup processes**. ChatGPT Enterprise is rolling out to **130,000+ employees globally**. Marc Casper stated: *"AI is shaping the future of science. And when combined with our mission to enable our customers to make the world healthier, cleaner and safer, it is a powerful catalyst to accelerate scientific advancement."* SVP and CIO Ryan Snyder emphasized in Forbes (March 2025): *"We didn't want AI to be a hammer looking for a nail. Instead, we treat it as part of a broader toolkit that includes automation and traditional machine learning."* The company had spent three years consolidating automation, AI, and data teams under unified leadership, establishing governance frameworks, and structuring AI strategy around three pillars: **operational efficiency, product/service enhancement, and customer experience**.

**Clario acquisition ($8.875B, announced October 2025, closing expected mid-2026).** This is transformative for PPD's data capture capabilities. Clario integrates clinical trial endpoint data from devices, sites, and patients, with solutions spanning **eCOA, medical imaging, cardiac solutions, respiratory assessments, and wearable data capture**. Its platform has supported approximately **70% of FDA drug approvals** over the past decade and **700+ regulatory approvals**. With ~4,000 employees and ~$1.25B in expected 2025 revenue, this acquisition adds the full endpoint data layer to PPD's stack. Casper called it *"an outstanding strategic fit, enabling faster, more informed drug development through differentiated technology and data intelligence solutions."* Expected synergies: **~$175M in adjusted operating income by year 5** and **~$0.45 adjusted EPS accretion in year 1**.

**Datavant collaboration (February 2026).** This partnership enables privacy-preserving tokenization and linkage of patient-level data across **350+ real-world data partners and 80,000 U.S. hospitals and clinics**. It covers RWD enrichment of randomized clinical trials, PPD CorEvitas registries, and PPD Evidera solutions. Karen Kaucic, M.D., President of Patient and Advisory Services and CMO of Clinical Research, stated: *"Data interoperability is critical to the next generation of evidence generation."* Krishna Cheriath, VP and Head of Digital and AI for Biopharma Services, added: *"This collaboration represents another milestone in our continued investment in digital transformation."*

## The "molecule-to-medicine" case rests on staggering waste in drug development

The data case for integrated digital clinical trials begins with the scale of inefficiency in drug development today. The average new drug takes **10–15 years** to develop and costs approximately **$2.2 billion** (Deloitte's 2024 figure for top-20 pharma companies, up from $2.12B in 2023). Clinical trials consume **68–70%** of total development cost, making them the single largest cost driver and the greatest opportunity for data-driven optimization.

Trial failure rates are at historic lows. The overall likelihood of approval from Phase I is just **6.7%** — an all-time low per Norstella/Citeline's 2014–2023 analysis. Phase II remains the biggest hurdle at only **~28% success**, while Phase III succeeds roughly **55%** of the time. A Phase III failure costs **$800 million to $1.4 billion**. The primary cause of failure — lack of clinical efficacy — accounts for **40–57%** of all Phase III failures, precisely the kind of failure that better patient selection, biomarker-driven enrollment, and real-time data analytics can prevent. Trials using **selection biomarkers increased overall likelihood of approval from 8% to 25%**, and Phase III success jumped from 55% to ~77% when biomarker-based patient selection was employed.

Patient recruitment is the dominant operational bottleneck. **Over 80% of clinical trials** fail to meet enrollment timelines. Only **6% of trials** complete on time. Up to **50% of investigator sites** enroll one patient or none. The updated cost of delay, per a 2024 Tufts CSDD empirical study, is approximately **$500,000/day in lost prescription drug sales** plus **$40,000–$56,000/day in direct trial operating costs** — with blockbuster drugs in cardiovascular and hematology losing $1.3–1.4M daily. Screen failure rates average **36.3%** and are rising.

Multi-vendor fragmentation compounds these problems. A typical global Phase III trial engages **20–50 vendors simultaneously**, and the clinical trial software ecosystem includes **400+ solutions across 20+ categories**. Manual reconciliation across fragmented systems consumes **10–20% of study staff time**. Protocol complexity has surged: average protocols now run **230 pages with ~175 procedures per patient** (up from 95 pages and 105 procedures previously), and data points collected per Phase III trial increased **283%** over the past decade. Unified platforms can reduce total cost of ownership by **28–42%** and shorten implementation timelines by **35–50%**.

## How PPD stacks up against IQVIA's data moat

The competitive comparison with IQVIA is the most important strategic context for any Data/Digital role at PPD. IQVIA is the undisputed leader in healthcare data and analytics. Understanding where PPD is strong, where it trails, and where the opportunity lies is essential.

**IQVIA's data moat is deep and largely irreplicable.** IQVIA possesses **1 billion+ de-identified patient records**, **30+ petabytes of data** from **140,000+ data suppliers**, spanning **45+ years** of historical healthcare data. Its Technology & Analytics Solutions (TAS) segment generated **$6.6 billion in FY2025 revenue** — growing 7.6% — a business with no direct analog at PPD or any other CRO. The E360 Real World Data Platform provides self-service access to this data with **250+ billion patient events**. IQVIA's R&DS segment generated **$8.9 billion** with a contracted backlog of **$32.7 billion** (book-to-bill of 1.12–1.18x). Total company revenue: **$16.3 billion in FY2025**, with guidance for **$17.2–17.4B in 2026**.

IQVIA's AI capability is production-grade. Through its NVIDIA partnership, IQVIA launched **IQVIA.ai** in March 2026 — a unified agentic AI platform with **150+ intelligent agents** deployed across clinical trial startup, data review, target identification, and field sales. It has **100+ AI-related patents filed**. Jensen Huang called it *"the world's largest automation platform for clinical trials."* **19 of the top 20 pharma companies** have begun incorporating IQVIA agents into their workflows.

**PPD's counter-positioning is vertical integration.** What PPD/Thermo Fisher can do that IQVIA cannot is integrate across the entire drug development continuum — from instruments and reagents through contract manufacturing (Patheon) through clinical trials (PPD) through real-world evidence (CorEvitas). The **Accelerator Drug Development** platform, launched Q4 2024, combines CRO + CDMO capabilities and has already engaged **700+ programs across 14 therapeutic areas** for **250+ customers**. A June 2025 Tufts CSDD study found integrated CRO/CDMO services can reduce development timelines by **up to 34 months** and generate **up to $63M in net financial benefits** per program (113x ROI). PPD worked on **10 of the top 10 best-selling drugs of 2022** and has conducted **2,800+ clinical trials** on **7,600+ compounds** in the past five years.

**CorEvitas anchors the RWE strategy.** Acquired in August 2023 for **$913 million**, CorEvitas has expanded from 12 to **15 independent registries** spanning **500+ investigator sites** with longitudinal data on **100,000+ patients**. Recent launches include registries for Alzheimer's disease (December 2025), myasthenia gravis (November 2025), systemic lupus erythematosus (February 2025), and an obesity registry tracking anti-obesity medications (January 2026). CorEvitas data is regulatory-grade and accepted for post-authorization safety studies.

## Conclusion: the strategic narrative for a Data/Digital role

The core narrative for a Data/Digital/Technology showcase at PPD should center on three themes. First, **PPD is at an inflection point** — the OpenAI, Clario, and Datavant deals represent the most concentrated digital investment in the division's history, and the role of data and technology professionals in executing this transformation is critical. Second, **integration is PPD's differentiator** — while IQVIA wins on data depth and AI maturity, no competitor can offer the molecule-to-medicine continuum that generates data across instruments, manufacturing, clinical supply, trial execution, and post-market evidence. The Accelerator platform's Tufts-validated 34-month timeline reduction is a powerful proof point. Third, **the market opportunity is immense and urgent** — with $500K/day lost to enrollment delays, 6.7% overall approval rates, and 283% growth in Phase III data points, the CRG that best unifies and activates clinical data wins disproportionate share in a $90B+ market growing 8–9% annually.
